Slab

A slab is a segment of network traffic that is routed through a VPN, often used for optimizing performance and security.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'slab' refers to a specific portion or segment of network traffic that is managed and transmitted via a Virtual Private Network (VPN). Slabs are used to efficiently handle data flow, ensuring that sensitive information is securely encrypted while optimizing bandwidth usage. By utilizing slabs, organizations can prioritize certain types of traffic, such as critical business applications, while maintaining the privacy and integrity of their data. This segmented approach allows for better management of network resources and can lead to improved performance, especially in environments where multiple applications and users are competing for bandwidth. Furthermore, slabs can help in compliance with data protection regulations by ensuring that only designated traffic passes through secure channels. The concept of slabs is particularly relevant in scenarios involving remote work, where diverse traffic types must be securely managed across different locations.

Examples

  • A company routes video conferencing traffic through a dedicated slab to ensure high-quality connections during remote meetings.
  • An organization segments its financial transaction data into a slab to maintain stringent security and compliance requirements.

Additional Information

  • Slabs can help in reducing latency for prioritized applications.
  • Using slabs can provide a clear overview of traffic patterns for better network management.
