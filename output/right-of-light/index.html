Right of light

A legal entitlement to receive natural light through a defined opening.

Description

The 'Right of Light' is a legal concept primarily found in property law, which grants property owners the right to receive natural light through windows or openings in their premises. This right can be established through long-term use, typically 20 years or more, and is often protected by laws that prevent neighboring properties from obstructing light. In urban settings, where buildings are in close proximity, the Right of Light can significantly influence construction and renovation projects. If a new building obstructs the light received by an existing property, the affected owner may have grounds to challenge the development. This legal right is important for ensuring that properties maintain their value and livability by preventing excessive shading from new constructions. In some cases, property owners may seek compensation or injunctions if their right to light is infringed upon. Understanding the Right of Light is crucial for architects, developers, and property owners when planning new projects or renovations in densely populated areas.

Examples

  • A homeowner successfully sues a developer for blocking their window light after a new high-rise is built next door.
  • A property owner negotiates an easement with a neighbor to maintain their Right of Light when planning a new construction.

Additional Information

  • The Right of Light can be acquired through prescription, meaning it can be gained by continuous use over a period.
  • In some jurisdictions, rights of light may be modified or extinguished through agreements or planning permissions.
