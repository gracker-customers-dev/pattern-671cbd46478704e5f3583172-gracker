Khatian

Khatian refers to a land record document in Bangladesh that contains details about land ownership and tenure.

Description

In the context of land management and agricultural policies in Bangladesh, a Khatian is an essential document that serves as a record of land ownership, rights, and cultivation. It plays a crucial role in establishing legal ownership and is used to settle disputes related to land. The Khatian contains information about the land's boundaries, the name of the owner, the type of land (such as agricultural or homestead), and the amount of land owned. It is often maintained by local government offices and is updated to reflect changes in ownership, such as sales or inheritance. The Khatian system is vital for effective land administration, ensuring that land rights are recognized and protected. In rural areas, having a Khatian is fundamental for farmers to access agricultural loans, government subsidies, and other benefits. The digitization of Khatian records has also been a significant step towards improving transparency and efficiency in land management practices in Bangladesh, facilitating better access to land information for stakeholders.

Examples

  • A farmer uses their Khatian to apply for a government subsidy for crop production.
  • In a land dispute, the Khatian is presented as proof of ownership in a local court.

Additional Information

  • The Khatian is often linked to the land survey system and cadastral maps.
  • Efforts are ongoing to digitize Khatian records to reduce corruption and enhance access.
