Community land trusts

Community land trusts (CLTs) are nonprofit organizations that hold land for the benefit of a community, ensuring affordable housing and sustainable land use.

Description

Community land trusts (CLTs) are innovative organizations that aim to promote community ownership and stewardship of land. By acquiring land and holding it in trust for the community, CLTs provide a mechanism to ensure that housing remains affordable and accessible to low- and moderate-income families. The land is typically leased to individuals or families who build homes on it, while the trust retains ownership of the land itself. This model helps to prevent displacement and speculation in real estate markets, allowing communities to maintain long-term housing stability. CLTs foster community engagement and decision-making, empowering residents to have a say in how land is used and developed. In addition to housing, community land trusts can support various community needs, such as agriculture, parks, and recreational facilities. By prioritizing communal interests over profit, CLTs contribute to more equitable and sustainable urban development.

Examples

  • The Champlain Housing Trust in Vermont, which focuses on affordable housing.
  • The Dudley Street Neighborhood Initiative in Boston, which integrates community development with land use.

Additional Information

  • CLTs can help combat gentrification and maintain cultural diversity in neighborhoods.
  • They often collaborate with local governments and organizations to enhance community resources.
