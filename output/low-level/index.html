Low level

Refers to basic or foundational aspects of VPN technology and its use cases.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'low level' refers to fundamental functionalities and basic implementations of Virtual Private Networks. These include core functions such as establishing secure connections between devices, encrypting data traffic, and ensuring privacy over the internet. Low-level VPN use cases typically involve direct connections between individual users and a VPN server, with minimal configuration or advanced features. These use cases are often utilized by individuals seeking to protect their online activities from eavesdropping, circumvent geographical content restrictions, or access secure networks remotely. Common scenarios for low-level VPN usage include accessing public Wi-Fi securely, maintaining privacy while browsing, and connecting to corporate networks from remote locations. Overall, low-level VPN use cases serve as the groundwork for more complex implementations and are essential for users who require basic privacy and security measures in their online activities.

Examples

  • Using a VPN to secure a connection on public Wi-Fi.
  • Accessing geo-restricted content from a different country.

Additional Information

  • Low-level VPNs often use protocols like PPTP or L2TP.
  • They are suitable for individual users rather than large organizations.
