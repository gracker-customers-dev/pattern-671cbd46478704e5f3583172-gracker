Cure period

A specified time frame allowing users to rectify issues related to VPN compliance or connectivity.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'cure period' refers to a designated timeframe granted to users or systems to address and resolve specific non-compliance issues or connectivity problems that may arise when using a Virtual Private Network. This period is crucial for maintaining security protocols and ensuring that users can regain compliance without immediate penalties or loss of access. For instance, if a user fails to connect securely or their device is flagged for non-compliance, the cure period allows them the opportunity to rectify these issues, such as updating their software or reconfiguring their settings. It serves as a buffer to enhance user experience while also ensuring that the organization's security policies are upheld. The duration of the cure period can vary based on the organization's policies and the nature of the compliance issue, but it is generally designed to be reasonable and effective in promoting adherence to security measures.

Examples

  • A user receives a notification about their VPN client being outdated and is given a 72-hour cure period to update it before access is revoked.
  • An organization allows employees a one-week cure period to resolve any device compliance issues identified during a routine security audit.

Additional Information

  • Cure periods can help reduce user frustration and support calls related to sudden access loss.
  • They are often part of broader compliance frameworks and security policies in organizations utilizing VPNs.
