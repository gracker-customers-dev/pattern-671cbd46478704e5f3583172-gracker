Cessation

The act of stopping or discontinuing VPN usage.

Description

Cessation in the context of VPN use cases refers to the termination of VPN connections or the complete discontinuation of VPN services. This can happen for various reasons, including changes in organizational policies, the end of a project that required secure remote access, or a shift to alternative security measures. For businesses, cessation of VPN usage may be driven by cost-benefit analyses, where maintaining a VPN is no longer justified compared to other security solutions. Additionally, users may cease using a VPN due to performance issues, lack of need for remote access, or dissatisfaction with the service provided. The process of cessation should be managed carefully to ensure that sensitive data remains secure, and that any potential vulnerabilities are addressed to protect the organization's network. Properly notifying users and providing alternatives or guidance on securing their connections post-cessation is also crucial.

Examples

  • An organization decides to stop using its VPN service after migrating to a cloud-based infrastructure with built-in security features.
  • A remote employee ceases to use their VPN after returning to the office full-time, as the need for secure remote access is no longer necessary.

Additional Information

  • Cessation may involve the decommissioning of VPN servers and infrastructure.
  • It is important to communicate policy changes related to VPN usage to all stakeholders to avoid confusion.
