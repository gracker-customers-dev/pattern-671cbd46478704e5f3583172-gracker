Incidental

Refers to the unintended or secondary use of a VPN beyond its primary purpose.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'incidental' refers to scenarios where users leverage VPN technology for purposes that were not originally intended or anticipated. While the primary use of a VPN is often to secure internet connections and protect privacy, incidental uses can arise from the flexibility and capabilities that VPNs offer. For instance, a user might originally set up a VPN to access corporate resources securely while working remotely. However, they may also find themselves using the VPN to bypass geo-restrictions on content, such as streaming services that are unavailable in their region. This incidental use highlights the versatility of VPNs, as they can serve multiple functions simultaneously, catering to the needs of the user in various contexts. As VPN technology evolves, users continue to discover new and unintended applications, making the concept of incidental use increasingly relevant in discussions about privacy and internet freedom.

Examples

  • Using a VPN primarily for secure remote work, but also accessing region-locked websites.
  • Setting up a VPN for secure browsing, only to discover faster connection speeds for certain online games.

Additional Information

  • Incidental uses may lead to unexpected legal or compliance issues.
  • Users should be aware of the terms of service of both the VPN provider and the services they access.
