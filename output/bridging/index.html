Bridging

Bridging in VPNs allows the extension of a local network over a VPN connection.

Description

Bridging is a technique used in Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) to connect two or more separate networks as if they are part of the same local network. This is particularly useful in scenarios where devices on different networks need to communicate seamlessly. By creating a bridge, VPNs can extend the broadcast domain, allowing devices to interact without requiring changes to their IP addressing. Bridging is commonly used in remote access VPNs, site-to-site VPNs, and scenarios where legacy systems need to connect with modern networks while preserving their original configurations. The bridging process encapsulates data packets and sends them over the VPN tunnel, thus creating a transparent link between the connected networks. This enables seamless access to resources, services, and applications across different physical locations, enhancing collaboration and operational efficiency. It is important to note that while bridging can simplify connectivity, it may introduce additional overhead and complexity in terms of network management and security considerations.

Examples

  • Connecting remote offices to a central office network as if they are on the same local network.
  • Enabling remote workers to access local printers and file shares without needing to reconfigure their devices.

Additional Information

  • Bridging may require specific configurations on routers and firewalls to allow broadcast traffic.
  • It can introduce latency due to the additional overhead of encapsulating and decapsulating packets.
