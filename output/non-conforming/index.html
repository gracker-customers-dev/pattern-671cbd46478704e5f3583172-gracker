Non conforming

Referring to VPN usage that does not adhere to established policies or standards.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'non conforming' refers to instances where the use of a Virtual Private Network deviates from the organization's defined security policies, compliance regulations, or best practices. This may occur when employees use unauthorized VPN services or configurations that compromise data security, such as unapproved encryption methods or connections to non-secure servers. Non conforming VPN use can expose an organization to various risks, including data breaches, malware infections, and regulatory penalties. The rise of remote work has exacerbated this issue, as employees may seek to bypass corporate controls for convenience or access to restricted content. Organizations need to implement strict guidelines and monitoring to ensure that VPN use aligns with their security frameworks, thereby protecting sensitive information and maintaining compliance with legal standards. Additionally, training employees on proper VPN usage and the implications of non conforming behavior can help mitigate risks.

Examples

  • An employee using a personal VPN service to access corporate resources instead of the approved company VPN.
  • Using a VPN that does not meet security standards, such as lacking strong encryption or logging policies.

Additional Information

  • Non conforming VPN usage can lead to significant security vulnerabilities.
  • Organizations should regularly audit VPN usage to identify and mitigate non conforming practices.
