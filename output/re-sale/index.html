Re sale

The act of selling a product or service that has been previously purchased.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 're sale' refers to the practice where a service provider purchases VPN services from a larger provider and then resells them to end-users or businesses. This model is common among smaller ISPs or IT service companies that do not have the infrastructure to develop their own VPN solutions. Resellers typically package VPN services with additional features or support to enhance value for their customers. This can include offering tailored solutions for specific business needs, such as secure remote access for employees, enhanced privacy protections, or bundled cybersecurity services. Resale can also include subscription models where the reseller manages customer billing and support, allowing the original provider to focus on service delivery. This model is advantageous for both parties: the original provider expands its market reach without incurring additional sales and marketing costs, while the reseller can offer a competitive service without the heavy investment in technology and infrastructure required to develop a VPN service from scratch.

Examples

  • An ISP that offers a branded VPN service using a larger VPN provider's infrastructure.
  • An IT consultancy that sells VPN subscriptions as part of a cybersecurity package for small businesses.

Additional Information

  • Reselling can lead to competitive pricing as resellers may offer discounts or promotions.
  • Resellers may provide localized support and customization options for their clients.
