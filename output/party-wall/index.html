Party wall

A shared boundary wall between two adjacent properties, commonly used in the context of data security and VPNs.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'party wall' refers to the conceptual boundary that separates the networks of two different entities or organizations. This term draws an analogy from real estate, where a party wall is a dividing wall shared by two buildings. In networking, particularly with the use of Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), the party wall represents the security measures and protocols that create a secure environment for data transmission between the two parties. VPNs establish encrypted tunnels that traverse this boundary, ensuring that data remains private and secure from external threats. The party wall serves as a critical point in network security, where firewalls, access controls, and encryption can be implemented to protect sensitive information. It is essential for organizations to understand the implications of having a party wall, especially when sharing resources, collaborating on projects, or conducting cross-organizational communications.

Examples

  • A company using a VPN to securely share files with a partner organization.
  • Two offices of the same company in different locations connecting through a VPN to maintain secure communications.

Additional Information

  • The concept of a party wall can also apply to cloud services where different tenants share the same infrastructure.
  • Understanding party walls is crucial for compliance with data protection regulations when sharing data across organizational boundaries.
