Fee simple absolute

A type of freehold interest in real property that grants the owner complete control and ownership rights.

Description

Fee simple absolute is a legal term primarily used in property law to describe the most complete form of ownership one can have over real estate. This concept grants the owner full rights to use, transfer, or sell the property without any limitations or conditions. In essence, the owner of a fee simple absolute has the greatest possible interest in the property, which lasts indefinitely and can be inherited. This form of ownership is not only beneficial for personal use but also allows for the potential generation of income through leasing or developing the property. It is important to note that fee simple absolute can be subject to zoning laws, covenants, and other regulations, but these do not affect the ownership rights inherent to the title. The flexibility of this ownership type makes it a common choice for individuals and businesses looking to invest in real estate.

Examples

  • A homeowner who purchases a house and lot outright holds a fee simple absolute.
  • A real estate investor who buys land for development has fee simple absolute ownership.

Additional Information

  • Fee simple absolute is distinct from leasehold estates, where ownership is temporary.
  • It is the most common form of property ownership in the United States.
