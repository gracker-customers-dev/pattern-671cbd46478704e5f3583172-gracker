Construction loan

A short-term loan specifically designed to finance the construction of a building or project.

Description

A construction loan is a type of short-term financing used to cover the costs associated with building a new structure or renovating an existing one. Unlike traditional mortgage loans, which are based on the value of the property, construction loans are based on the projected future value of the completed project. These loans typically have higher interest rates and are disbursed in stages, or 'draws', as construction progresses. Borrowers usually need to provide a detailed construction plan and timeline, along with a budget, to secure a construction loan. The lender will often require inspections at each stage of construction to ensure that the work is progressing as planned before releasing the next draw of funds. Once the construction is complete, the loan can either be paid off or converted into a permanent mortgage. This type of financing is crucial for builders and developers as it provides the necessary capital to complete projects in a timely manner.

Examples

  • A contractor takes out a construction loan to build a new residential complex.
  • A homeowner secures a construction loan to renovate their kitchen and bathroom.

Additional Information

  • Construction loans typically have a term of 6 to 12 months.
  • Interest payments are usually made during the construction period, with principal repayment starting afterward.
