Public limited

A public limited company (PLC) is a type of company whose shares are traded on the stock exchange and can be bought by the public.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'public limited' company refers to organizations that are publicly traded and typically operate with a higher level of regulatory scrutiny and transparency. These companies may utilize VPNs to facilitate secure remote access for employees, protect sensitive financial data, and ensure compliance with data protection regulations. A VPN can help a public limited company secure communications between its various branches and remote workers, safeguarding against data breaches and cyber threats. Furthermore, the use of VPNs can assist in maintaining the confidentiality of proprietary information, ensuring that sensitive corporate communications are encrypted and shielded from unauthorized access. For public limited companies, which often handle large volumes of customer and financial data, the implementation of VPNs can be a crucial component of their overall cybersecurity strategy, helping to build trust with investors and stakeholders by demonstrating a commitment to data security and privacy.

Examples

  • Facilitating secure remote work arrangements for employees of a public limited company.
  • Protecting sensitive financial transactions during online communications.

Additional Information

  • Public limited companies may face stricter compliance requirements, making VPN use essential for data protection.
  • VPNs can help public limited companies maintain competitive advantages by securing proprietary information.
