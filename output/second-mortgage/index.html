Second mortgage

A second mortgage is a loan taken out against a property that is already mortgaged, allowing the homeowner to access additional funds.

Description

A second mortgage is a type of loan secured against a property that already has an existing first mortgage. This financial tool allows homeowners to borrow against the equity they have built up in their home. The second mortgage can be used for various purposes, such as home improvements, debt consolidation, or funding major expenses like education or medical bills. The amount available for a second mortgage is typically determined by the difference between the property's current market value and the outstanding balance of the first mortgage. It is important to note that because a second mortgage is subordinate to the first mortgage, in the event of foreclosure, the first mortgage lender takes precedence. As such, second mortgages often come with higher interest rates than first mortgages due to the increased risk to the lender. Homeowners considering a second mortgage should carefully evaluate their financial situation and the implications of adding more debt against their home.

Examples

  • Using a second mortgage to finance a home renovation project.
  • Consolidating high-interest debts through a second mortgage.

Additional Information

  • A second mortgage can come in the form of a home equity loan or a home equity line of credit (HELOC).
  • Defaulting on a second mortgage can lead to foreclosure on the property.
