Transfer development rights

A mechanism allowing the transfer of unused development potential from one property to another.

Description

Transfer development rights (TDR) is a land use regulation tool that allows property owners to sell or transfer the rights to develop their property to another location. This is often used in urban planning to promote conservation and manage growth effectively. For instance, a property owner in a designated preservation area may sell their development rights to a developer in a more suitable area, allowing the developer to increase the density of a project while protecting the environmental integrity of the preservation area. TDR programs are often employed in regions experiencing rapid urbanization, where preserving open space and historical sites is essential. By enabling the transfer of development rights, municipalities can guide development to areas that can accommodate growth while safeguarding areas of ecological, historical, or cultural significance. TDR can also provide financial benefits to property owners who sell their rights, contributing to the economic viability of preservation efforts.

Examples

  • A farmer sells development rights to a nearby urban developer, preserving his farmland while allowing for increased urban density.
  • In a historic district, property owners may transfer their development rights to a developer planning a new project outside the district, thus preserving the character of the historic area.

Additional Information

  • TDR programs can be used in conjunction with zoning laws to enhance urban planning.
  • Successful TDR programs require clear guidelines and effective monitoring to ensure compliance and protect public interests.
