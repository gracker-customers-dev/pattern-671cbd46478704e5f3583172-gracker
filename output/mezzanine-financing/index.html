Mezzanine financing

A hybrid form of financing that combines debt and equity characteristics, often used to finance the expansion of existing companies.

Description

Mezzanine financing is a type of capital that is used to fund the growth of a company, typically in the form of subordinated debt or preferred equity. It sits between senior debt and equity in the capital structure, meaning it is riskier than senior debt but less risky than equity. This type of financing is commonly used by companies that are looking to expand but may not have sufficient collateral to secure traditional bank loans. Mezzanine financing often comes with higher interest rates compared to senior loans and may involve equity kickers, which means lenders may receive a portion of the company's equity if the business succeeds. This financing is particularly favored in leveraged buyouts, growth capital, and recapitalizations. In the context of Virtual Private Network (VPN) use cases, companies may seek mezzanine financing to invest in technology infrastructure, enhance cybersecurity measures, or expand their service offerings to meet growing customer demand in a competitive market.

Examples

  • A tech startup seeking mezzanine financing to expand its VPN service offerings and improve infrastructure.
  • A cybersecurity firm using mezzanine financing to enhance its VPN security features and grow its market share.

Additional Information

  • Mezzanine financing can provide faster access to capital compared to traditional equity financing.
  • It is often used by companies that have established cash flows but need additional capital for growth.
