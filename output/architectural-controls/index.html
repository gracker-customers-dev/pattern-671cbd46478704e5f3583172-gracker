Architectural controls

Measures and protocols designed to manage and secure VPN architectures.

Description

Architectural controls refer to the strategic design and implementation of protocols, structures, and policies that govern the use and security of Virtual Private Networks (VPNs). These controls are essential for ensuring that VPNs provide secure communication channels while maintaining organizational integrity and compliance with regulations. Architectural controls encompass a range of elements, including the configuration of network devices, the segmentation of networks, access control mechanisms, and the establishment of security policies that dictate how data is transmitted over VPNs. They also involve the selection of VPN technologies (such as IPSec, SSL/TLS) that align with the organizational security architecture. By implementing architectural controls, organizations can mitigate risks associated with data breaches, unauthorized access, and ensure that VPN connections are resilient against attacks. Furthermore, these controls help in maintaining high availability and reliability of VPN services, which are crucial for remote work and secure connectivity for users across various locations.

Examples

  • Implementation of multi-factor authentication for VPN access.
  • Use of split tunneling to control traffic flow and protect sensitive data.

Additional Information

  • Regular audits and assessments of VPN configurations to identify vulnerabilities.
  • Integration of VPN solutions with existing security information and event management (SIEM) systems.
