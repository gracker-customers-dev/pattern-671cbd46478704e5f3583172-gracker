Community

A group of users or devices sharing a common interest or purpose within a VPN.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'Community' refers to a defined group of users, devices, or networks that share specific resources and communication needs while connected through a Virtual Private Network. This concept is essential in establishing secure, controlled access to information and services among members of the community. For instance, a corporate environment may define a community that includes employees, contractors, and partners who need to access internal systems and data securely. Communities can be formed based on various criteria such as organizational roles, geographic locations, or project affiliations. By segmenting users into communities, organizations can apply tailored security policies, manage bandwidth more effectively, and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements. This segmentation enhances the overall efficiency and security of network communications. Additionally, communities can leverage shared resources like applications and databases, promoting collaboration and productivity while maintaining a secure environment.

Examples

  • A corporate VPN community that includes employees and trusted third-party vendors.
  • A school VPN community for students and faculty to access educational resources.

Additional Information

  • Communities can be configured with specific access controls and permissions.
  • Different communities can have varying levels of security protocols based on sensitivity.
