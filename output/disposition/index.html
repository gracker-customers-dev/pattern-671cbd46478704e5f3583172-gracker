Disposition

The manner in which a VPN connection is managed or terminated.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'disposition' refers to the way a VPN session is concluded or the state of the VPN connection once it is no longer active. This can include various scenarios, such as automatic disconnection after a specified time period, user-initiated termination of the session, or the VPN software's response to network changes or failures. Proper management of VPN disposition is essential for ensuring security, as it can help mitigate risks associated with unauthorized access to sensitive data once the VPN session ends. It also affects the user experience, as seamless connection and disconnection processes are crucial for maintaining productivity. The disposition of a VPN connection can also involve logging and reporting processes that track session details for compliance purposes. Effective disposition strategies can enhance the overall security posture of an organization by ensuring that data remains protected even when connections are no longer active.

Examples

  • A VPN automatically disconnects after 30 minutes of inactivity.
  • A user manually ends their VPN session after completing a work task.

Additional Information

  • Disposition policies can be configured to enhance security.
  • Logging session disposition can aid in compliance and auditing.
