Landing

The process of establishing a secure connection to a remote network via a VPN.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'Landing' refers to the initial phase in which a user or device connects to a virtual private network (VPN) to access resources securely. This process typically involves authentication and the establishment of an encrypted tunnel that protects data as it travels across potentially insecure networks, such as the internet. Landing is crucial for ensuring that sensitive information, such as personal data or corporate files, remains confidential and secure from eavesdropping or other cyber threats. Once the VPN connection is established, users can access internal networks, applications, and services as if they were directly connected to the local network. This capability is particularly valuable for remote workers, allowing them to securely connect to their company's resources from anywhere in the world. Effective landing processes often include multi-factor authentication and robust encryption protocols to enhance security and ensure that only authorized users can access the network.

Examples

  • Remote employees connecting to corporate networks while working from home.
  • Traveling professionals accessing company resources securely from public Wi-Fi.

Additional Information

  • Landing can involve various authentication methods, including passwords, tokens, or biometric data.
  • The quality of the VPN connection can affect the performance of applications accessed after landing.
