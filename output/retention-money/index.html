Retention money

A portion of payment withheld until the completion of a project to ensure contract fulfillment.

Description

Retention money refers to a specific amount of money that is withheld from the total payment due to a contractor or service provider until specific conditions are met, often related to the completion of a project. In the context of contracts, particularly in construction or service agreements, retention is used as a safeguard for the client to ensure that the contractor completes the work to the required standards. This practice helps to ensure that contractors do not cut corners in order to complete a project quickly. Retention money is usually a percentage of the total contract value, and it is released only after the project has been completed, inspected, and approved. The duration for which the retention is held can vary, often depending on the type of project and the terms agreed upon in the contract. Retention money serves as an incentive for the contractor to address any defects or issues that may arise after project completion, thereby protecting the client's interests.

Examples

  • In construction contracts, 5% of the total contract value may be retained until the final inspection.
  • In software development, a client may hold back 10% of payment until the software passes all acceptance tests.

Additional Information

  • Retention money can help mitigate risks associated with project completion and quality.
  • The terms of retention money should be clearly defined in the contract to avoid disputes.
