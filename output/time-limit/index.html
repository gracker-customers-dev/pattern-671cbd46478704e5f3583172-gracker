Time limit

A specified duration during which a VPN connection can be active or a user can access certain resources.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'time limit' refers to the predefined period that a user is allowed to maintain a VPN connection or access particular services. This concept is crucial for organizations that need to manage bandwidth, enhance security, or enforce compliance with policies. Setting a time limit can help reduce the risk of unauthorized access, as users may only be allowed to connect to the VPN during designated hours or for a set duration. For example, a company may allow remote employees to access their internal network only during business hours, or a client may have a time-limited access to a server for specific tasks. Time limits can also assist in resource allocation, ensuring that network resources are used efficiently and that connections do not remain open longer than necessary. This practice is common in corporate environments, educational institutions, and any organization that values security and optimal resource management.

Examples

  • An employee can access the corporate VPN only from 9 AM to 5 PM.
  • A contractor is granted a VPN connection with a time limit of 30 days.

Additional Information

  • Time limits can be enforced through VPN software configurations.
  • Users may receive notifications before their connection expires.
