Stand by

A state in which a VPN connection is prepared to be activated quickly without needing to reconnect.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'Stand by' refers to a mode where the VPN client is not actively connected to the VPN server but is ready to establish a secure connection with minimal delay. This is particularly useful for users who need quick access to secure resources without going through the full connection process each time. For instance, a remote worker might leave their VPN in 'stand by' mode to ensure that they can access company resources securely as soon as they need to, without waiting for the VPN to re-establish a connection. This mode can also be beneficial for mobile users who frequently switch between networks (e.g., Wi-Fi to cellular), allowing them to maintain a level of security while ensuring fast reconnection to their VPN. However, it is essential for users to be aware of the security implications when a VPN is not actively connected, as data may be exposed to local networks.

Examples

  • A remote employee leaves their VPN on stand by while traveling to quickly connect to the office network when needed.
  • A mobile user keeps the VPN client in stand by mode to ensure a seamless transition between Wi-Fi and cellular data without losing security.

Additional Information

  • Stand by mode helps reduce the time needed for reconnection, improving user experience.
  • Users should ensure that their VPN client has robust security measures in place even when not actively connected.
