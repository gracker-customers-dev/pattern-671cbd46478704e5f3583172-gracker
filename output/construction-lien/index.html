Construction lien

A legal claim against a property for unpaid construction work.

Description

A construction lien, also known as a mechanic's lien, is a powerful legal tool used by contractors, subcontractors, and suppliers to secure payment for work performed or materials supplied on a construction project. When a contractor or supplier does not receive payment for their services, they may file a construction lien against the property where the work was done. This lien serves as a public notice of their claim and can potentially lead to a legal process whereby the property may be sold to satisfy the debt. The process of filing a construction lien typically involves meeting specific legal requirements, such as providing notice to the property owner and filing within a certain timeframe. Construction liens are governed by state laws, which can vary significantly, impacting the rights and obligations of both the lien claimant and the property owner. This mechanism helps ensure that those who contribute labor or materials to a construction project can seek legal recourse to secure payment, thereby promoting fairness in the construction industry.

Examples

  • A contractor files a lien on a homeowner's property for unpaid renovations.
  • A supplier files a lien against a commercial building for materials delivered but not paid for.

Additional Information

  • Construction liens can lead to foreclosure if not resolved.
  • Each state has specific rules regarding the filing and enforcement of construction liens.
