Bonded

The process of combining multiple internet connections to create a single, faster, and more reliable VPN connection.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'bonded' refers to the technique of aggregating multiple internet connections, such as DSL, cable, and cellular, to increase overall bandwidth and improve redundancy. This is particularly useful for users who require a stable and fast connection for activities such as video streaming, online gaming, or remote work. By using bonding technology, data packets can be split and transmitted over multiple connections simultaneously, which enhances the speed of data transfer and provides a failover mechanism if one connection goes down. Bonding can help organizations optimize their network performance and ensure that critical applications remain accessible even during high traffic or connection failures. Additionally, bonded connections can be advantageous in locations where a single internet service provider may not offer sufficient bandwidth, enabling users to leverage the combined potential of multiple providers.

Examples

  • A remote team using bonded connections for videoconferencing to ensure smooth communication without interruptions.
  • A live-streaming event utilizing bonded internet connections to maintain high-quality video feeds from multiple locations.

Additional Information

  • Bonding can be achieved using hardware solutions or software applications that manage multiple connections.
  • Organizations often use bonded VPNs to enhance performance for critical applications, especially in remote or mobile work scenarios.
