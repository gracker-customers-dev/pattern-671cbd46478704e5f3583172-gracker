Stop

A term indicating the action of terminating a VPN connection.

Description

In the context of VPN (Virtual Private Network) use cases, 'Stop' refers to the action of disconnecting from a VPN service. This can be done for various reasons, such as when a user no longer requires a secure connection, wishes to revert to their standard internet connection, or needs to troubleshoot connectivity issues. Stopping a VPN connection effectively ceases all encrypted routing of internet traffic through the VPN server, allowing the user's device to communicate directly with the internet service provider (ISP). This action can be performed via the VPN client interface, where users typically have the option to connect or disconnect. Additionally, stopping a VPN may be necessary when switching networks (e.g., from Wi-Fi to mobile data) or when encountering issues such as reduced internet speed or connection instability. Importantly, users should be aware of the implications of stopping a VPN, particularly in terms of privacy and security, as their internet activity will no longer be protected by the VPN's encryption.

Examples

  • A user stops their VPN connection to access local content that is restricted while connected to the VPN.
  • An employee disconnects from the corporate VPN after finishing their work to return to regular browsing.

Additional Information

  • Stopping a VPN can expose the user's actual IP address and location.
  • Many modern VPN services offer 'kill switch' features that automatically stop internet traffic if the VPN connection drops unexpectedly.
