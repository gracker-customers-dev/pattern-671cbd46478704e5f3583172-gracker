Hold period analysis

A method for evaluating the duration of VPN usage for specific use cases.

Description

Hold period analysis is a critical evaluation technique used in the context of VPN (Virtual Private Network) use cases to assess how long users maintain their VPN connections for various activities. This analysis helps organizations understand user behavior, identify patterns in VPN usage, and optimize the performance and security of their VPN services. By examining the hold periods, businesses can determine the effectiveness of their VPN solutions in meeting user needs, such as secure access to corporate resources, privacy while browsing, or bypassing geo-restrictions. It can also provide insights into peak usage times, which can inform resource allocation and capacity planning. Additionally, analyzing hold periods can assist in identifying unusual patterns that may indicate security threats, such as prolonged connections during non-business hours. Overall, hold period analysis is a valuable tool for enhancing VPN strategies and ensuring they align with organizational goals and user expectations.

Examples

  • Analyzing the average hold period for employees accessing company resources remotely.
  • Identifying peak usage times based on hold periods during specific business hours.

Additional Information

  • Can help in optimizing server load balancing based on user connection times.
  • Useful for compliance and auditing purposes to track VPN usage patterns.
