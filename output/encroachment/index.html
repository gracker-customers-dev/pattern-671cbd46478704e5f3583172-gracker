Encroachment

Unintended or unauthorized access to a network or system due to the use of VPNs.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, encroachment refers to the phenomenon where users, often through the use of Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), gain access to restricted or sensitive areas of a network that they are not authorized to access. This can occur when employees connect to a company’s internal network from remote locations, utilizing VPN technology to bypass security measures that distinguish between permitted and non-permitted access. Encroachment can lead to serious security risks, including data breaches, unauthorized data manipulation, or exposure to sensitive information. Organizations must implement strict access controls and monitoring to mitigate the risks associated with encroachment, ensuring that only authorized personnel can access critical systems and data. Additionally, organizations should provide clear guidelines and training on the appropriate use of VPNs to help prevent accidental encroachment and maintain the integrity of their network security posture.

Examples

  • An employee using a personal VPN to bypass company firewall restrictions and access sensitive internal databases.
  • A contractor inadvertently accessing a secure network segment while connected to the company VPN without proper authorization.

Additional Information

  • Regular audits of VPN access logs can help detect encroachment incidents.
  • Implementing role-based access controls can reduce the risk of encroachment through VPNs.
