Combined

A VPN setup that integrates multiple functionalities or protocols to enhance security and performance.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, the term 'Combined' refers to a configuration that utilizes more than one VPN type or combines different technologies to achieve a more robust and efficient virtual private network solution. This often involves the integration of different VPN protocols (such as OpenVPN, L2TP/IPsec, and IKEv2) to leverage their unique advantages. For instance, a combined setup might use a secure tunneling protocol alongside an encryption protocol to enhance data confidentiality and integrity. Additionally, combined VPNs may serve various purposes such as remote access and site-to-site connectivity, allowing organizations to secure their data transmissions across multiple channels. This approach not only improves security by providing layered defenses but also optimizes performance by routing traffic according to specific needs. As cyber threats evolve, combined VPN setups are increasingly essential for businesses seeking to maintain secure communications while ensuring reliable access to resources.

Examples

  • Using OpenVPN for remote access while employing IPsec for site-to-site connections.
  • Implementing a combined VPN solution that incorporates SSL and IKEv2 protocols for enhanced security.

Additional Information

  • Combined VPNs can offer flexibility in deployment for varying user needs.
  • They may require more complex configurations and management compared to single-protocol VPNs.
