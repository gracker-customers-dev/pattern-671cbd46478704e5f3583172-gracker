Straight

A direct connection method in VPN usage without additional routing or tunneling.

Description

In the context of VPN (Virtual Private Network) use cases, the term 'Straight' refers to a direct and unencumbered connection between a user's device and the VPN server. This type of connection typically does not involve any complex routing or tunneling techniques that might be employed in more intricate VPN configurations. Users may opt for a 'Straight' connection when they require a simple and efficient means to connect to a secure network, often for straightforward tasks such as accessing remote work resources or securing their internet traffic. This approach can lead to faster speeds and lower latency, as the data does not undergo extensive processing or rerouting. However, it may not provide the same level of obfuscation or anonymity as other more complex VPN setups. 'Straight' connections are particularly useful for users who prioritize speed and ease of use over advanced features.

Examples

  • Accessing corporate resources with minimal latency.
  • Simple internet browsing over a secure connection.

Additional Information

  • Ideal for users with basic VPN needs.
  • May not offer advanced features like IP masking.
