Informal settlement

A type of settlement lacking official recognition and typically characterized by inadequate infrastructure and services.

Description

Informal settlements are residential areas that have not been formally recognized by local authorities. These settlements often arise due to rapid urbanization, where individuals migrate to cities in search of better opportunities but cannot afford formal housing. As a result, they establish makeshift homes on land that is often unregulated or illegally occupied. Informal settlements are characterized by a lack of basic services such as clean water, sanitation, and electricity. The residents of these areas frequently face vulnerabilities, including eviction risks, limited access to healthcare, and inadequate educational facilities. Informal settlements can vary significantly in terms of size, location, and the degree of organization among residents. The existence of these settlements poses challenges for urban planning and governance, as they often fall outside the jurisdiction of local authorities. Additionally, in the context of VPN use cases, individuals in informal settlements may rely on VPNs to access information, communicate securely, and protect their privacy, especially in environments where internet access is restricted or monitored.

Examples

  • Kibera in Nairobi, Kenya, is one of the largest informal settlements in Africa.
  • Dharavi in Mumbai, India, is a well-known informal settlement with a vibrant community.

Additional Information

  • Informal settlements often lack legal property rights, making residents vulnerable to displacement.
  • Community-driven initiatives are sometimes established to improve living conditions and advocate for residents' rights.
