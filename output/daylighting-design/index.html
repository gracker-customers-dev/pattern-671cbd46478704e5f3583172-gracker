Daylighting design

The strategic use of natural light in architectural design to enhance indoor environments.

Description

Daylighting design refers to the practice of utilizing natural light to illuminate interior spaces, reducing reliance on artificial lighting and improving energy efficiency. This design approach involves careful planning of windows, skylights, and reflective surfaces to maximize the penetration of sunlight into buildings. Effective daylighting can enhance the aesthetic quality of a space, improve occupant mood and productivity, and reduce energy costs associated with lighting. In the context of building and construction, daylighting design considers factors such as the building's orientation, the geographic location, and the surrounding environment to optimize light quality and quantity throughout the day and seasons. By integrating daylighting strategies, architects and designers aim to create healthier, more sustainable buildings that leverage the benefits of natural light while minimizing glare and overheating. The overall goal is to balance daylight availability with the need for privacy and thermal comfort, leading to a better living and working environment.

Examples

  • Using clerestory windows to enhance light in high-ceiling spaces.
  • Incorporating light shelves to reflect daylight deeper into the building.

Additional Information

  • Daylighting can significantly reduce energy consumption in buildings.
  • Effective daylighting can lead to improved occupant well-being and productivity.
