Forward

The act of routing or directing network traffic through a VPN to a specified destination.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'Forward' refers to the process of directing data packets from one point to another via a Virtual Private Network. This usually involves sending traffic from a client device through an encrypted tunnel to a VPN server, which then forwards the traffic to its intended destination on the internet. This forward mechanism is essential for maintaining user privacy and security, as it masks the user's IP address and encrypts the data being transmitted. Forwarding can occur in various scenarios, such as accessing geo-restricted content, securing communications over public Wi-Fi, or allowing remote workers to securely connect to their company’s internal network. Additionally, it can also refer to the forwarding of traffic between different networks, where a VPN acts as an intermediary to ensure secure and private communication. Overall, forwarding in a VPN context enhances both security and accessibility for users.

Examples

  • Accessing a streaming service that is only available in certain regions by routing traffic through a VPN server located in that region.
  • Allowing remote employees to connect to their organization’s intranet securely by forwarding their internet traffic through a VPN.

Additional Information

  • Forwarding helps in bypassing internet censorship by routing traffic through servers in unrestricted locations.
  • VPNs may offer features like split tunneling, allowing users to forward specific traffic through the VPN while keeping other traffic local.
