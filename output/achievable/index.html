Achievable

Capable of being accomplished or attained within the context of VPN use cases.

Description

In the context of VPN (Virtual Private Network) use cases, 'achievable' refers to the realistic and practical goals that organizations and individuals can set when implementing a VPN solution. These goals may include ensuring secure remote access to company resources, protecting sensitive data during transmission, enabling secure communication across public networks, and complying with regulatory requirements. The achievable nature of these goals depends on factors such as the technology in use, the organization's infrastructure, user requirements, and budget constraints. For instance, a company may aim to have 100% of its remote employees securely connected to its internal network within a specific timeframe. However, the feasibility of this goal is influenced by the VPN's scalability, user training, and technical support. Ultimately, achievable VPN goals are those that can be effectively planned and executed to enhance security and productivity without compromising on performance.

Examples

  • Enabling remote employees to securely access internal applications and resources.
  • Establishing a site-to-site VPN for secure communication between branch offices.

Additional Information

  • Achievable goals should be specific, measurable, and time-bound.
  • Regular assessments and updates can help ensure that VPN objectives remain achievable over time.
