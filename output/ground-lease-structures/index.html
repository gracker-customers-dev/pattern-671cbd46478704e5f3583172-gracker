Ground lease structures

Ground lease structures refer to long-term leases where the landowner leases the land to a tenant, who can develop it while the land remains owned by the lessor.

Description

Ground lease structures are a unique form of real estate leasing that allows a tenant to occupy and develop land owned by another party (the lessor). Typically, these leases are long-term, often spanning 50 to 99 years. The tenant, known as the lessee, can build structures or make improvements on the land, but ownership of the land itself remains with the lessor. At the end of the lease term, ownership of all improvements usually reverts to the landowner. This arrangement is advantageous for both parties: the landowner receives a steady income without the responsibility of property management, while the tenant can develop property without a significant upfront cost of purchasing land. Ground leases are common in commercial real estate, particularly for developments in urban areas where land costs are high. They can also be used strategically by businesses to minimize capital expenditures and leverage financing options for development.

Examples

  • A hotel chain leases land from a city to build a hotel, with the lease structured as a ground lease.
  • A retail development on leased land where the retailer builds a store while paying rent to the landowner.

Additional Information

  • Ground leases can be structured to allow for periodic rent adjustments based on market rates.
  • They are often seen as a way to facilitate development in high-value areas while maintaining land ownership.
