Original cost

The initial expense incurred to implement a VPN solution.

Description

Original cost refers to the total expenditure associated with setting up a Virtual Private Network (VPN) from scratch. This cost encompasses various components such as hardware (routers, firewalls, and servers), software licenses, installation services, and any necessary infrastructure enhancements. When organizations consider adopting a VPN, evaluating the original cost is essential to understand the financial commitment involved. This upfront investment is crucial for ensuring secure remote access for employees, protecting sensitive data, and facilitating secure communications across different geographical locations. The original cost can vary widely depending on the scale of the VPN deployment, the technology chosen (such as MPLS, IPsec, or SSL), and the specific requirements of the organization. Moreover, organizations must also account for ongoing costs such as maintenance, support, and potential upgrades, which can impact the total cost of ownership over time. Understanding the original cost helps in budgeting, planning, and justifying the investment in VPN solutions.

Examples

  • Initial investment for hardware and software needed to establish a VPN connection.
  • Costs associated with consulting services for VPN setup and configuration.

Additional Information

  • Original cost does not include ongoing operational expenses.
  • Comparing original costs of different VPN solutions helps in making informed decisions.
