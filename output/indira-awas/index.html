Indira Awas

Indira Awas refers to a housing scheme aimed at providing affordable housing to the rural poor in India.

Description

Indira Awas Yojana (IAY) was a government initiative launched in India in 1999 to provide financial assistance for the construction of houses for the economically weaker sections of society, particularly in rural areas. The program was part of the larger effort to improve living conditions and reduce poverty in India. Beneficiaries are typically from scheduled castes, scheduled tribes, and below the poverty line (BPL) families. Under this scheme, the government provides a subsidy or direct financial aid to help families construct their own homes. The scheme has contributed significantly to increasing the housing stock in rural areas and improving the quality of life for many. In 2016, IAY was subsumed under the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY), which expanded the scope of affordable housing initiatives to urban areas as well, focusing on providing housing for all by 2022. The program emphasizes self-construction and encourages the use of local materials and skills.

Examples

  • Provision of financial aid for building houses in rural villages.
  • Support for families from economically weaker sections to secure housing.

Additional Information

  • Part of India's broader poverty alleviation and housing initiative.
  • Transitioned into the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana in 2016.
