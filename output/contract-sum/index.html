Contract sum

The total amount agreed upon in a contract for performance or services rendered.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, the 'contract sum' refers to the total financial commitment made by an organization to utilize a Virtual Private Network service. This sum encompasses all costs associated with the VPN, including subscription fees, setup costs, maintenance, and any additional services required for optimal performance. As organizations increasingly turn to VPNs to secure remote access to their networks, understanding the contract sum becomes essential for budgeting and financial planning. The contract sum may vary based on factors such as the number of users, level of encryption, bandwidth requirements, and duration of the contract. Additionally, it can also include provisions for scalability, which allows organizations to adjust their VPN usage as their needs change over time. Accurately determining the contract sum is crucial for organizations to ensure they receive the necessary services while managing their expenses effectively.

Examples

  • A company signs a one-year VPN service contract for $12,000, covering 100 users.
  • An enterprise negotiates a multi-year contract with a VPN provider that totals $50,000 including setup and ongoing support.

Additional Information

  • Contract sums can vary widely depending on service level agreements (SLAs) and features.
  • Organizations should compare different VPN providers to ensure they get the best value for the contract sum.
