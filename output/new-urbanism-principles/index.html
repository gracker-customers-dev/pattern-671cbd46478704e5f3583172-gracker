New urbanism principles

A movement promoting walkable neighborhoods and sustainable urban design.

Description

New Urbanism is an urban design movement that emerged in the late 20th century aimed at creating walkable neighborhoods that foster community interaction and promote sustainability. The principles focus on mixed-use development, where residential, commercial, and recreational spaces are integrated. This approach encourages the use of public transportation, reduces dependency on automobiles, and enhances the quality of life for residents by providing accessible amenities within walking distance. New Urbanism emphasizes the importance of public spaces, architecture that reflects local culture, and the preservation of green spaces. The movement seeks to counteract urban sprawl and the negative impacts of car-centric planning by promoting dense, pedestrian-friendly environments. In contemporary discussions, these principles can also intersect with technology, such as the use of Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), which can support remote work and connectivity in these walkable communities, enabling residents to maintain their urban lifestyle while accessing global networks securely.

Examples

  • Seaside, Florida - a quintessential New Urbanist community designed for walkability and accessibility.
  • Portland, Oregon - known for its urban growth boundary and mixed-use development that supports public transit.

Additional Information

  • New Urbanism advocates for a variety of housing types to accommodate diverse populations.
  • It promotes environmental sustainability by encouraging efficient land use and reducing carbon footprints.
