Federal

Relating to the national government or a system of government that divides powers between central and regional authorities.

Description

In the context of VPN (Virtual Private Network) use cases, 'Federal' refers to the use of VPNs by government entities, particularly at the federal level, to secure communications and protect sensitive data. Federal agencies often handle classified information and must comply with strict regulations regarding data security and privacy. VPNs provide a secure tunnel for transmitting data over the internet, which is crucial for agencies that need to ensure confidentiality and integrity of their communications. Federal VPN use cases include secure remote access for employees working from various locations, secure inter-agency communications, and protection of data transmitted between federal facilities. These VPNs must meet specific compliance standards, such as FIPS (Federal Information Processing Standards) and NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology) guidelines, to ensure they are secure enough for government use. The implementation of federal VPNs is essential for maintaining national security and enabling efficient and secure collaboration among different government departments.

Examples

  • Secure remote access for federal employees to sensitive databases.
  • Encrypted communication between federal agencies for coordinated responses.

Additional Information

  • Federal VPNs must comply with FIPS and NIST standards.
  • They often incorporate advanced security features, such as multi-factor authentication.
