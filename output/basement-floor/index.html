Basement floor

The baseline level of security and performance expected from a VPN.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, the term 'Basement floor' refers to the minimum acceptable standards for security, performance, and usability that a Virtual Private Network must meet to be considered viable for deployment. This includes factors such as encryption strength, connection reliability, speed, and the ability to bypass geo-restrictions or firewalls. Organizations often set this baseline to ensure that any VPN service they use will adequately protect their data and maintain productivity. The basement floor serves as a threshold; any VPN solution that does not meet these standards may compromise user security or hinder business operations. As cyber threats evolve, the basement floor may also shift over time, necessitating regular assessments and updates to the VPN policies and technologies in place. Ultimately, establishing a basement floor helps organizations avoid inadequate VPN solutions and ensures a consistent level of protection for sensitive information transmitted over the internet.

Examples

  • A company requires a VPN that uses at least AES-256 encryption for data protection.
  • An organization mandates that any VPN must have a minimum uptime of 99.9% for reliable access.

Additional Information

  • The basement floor may vary between different industries based on regulatory requirements.
  • Regular security audits can help reassess and elevate the basement floor as needed.
