Commercial paper

A short-term unsecured promissory note issued by a corporation.

Description

Commercial paper is a financial instrument used by corporations to obtain short-term funding. It typically has maturities that range from a few days to a year and is issued at a discount to face value. Companies use commercial paper to finance their working capital needs, such as inventory purchases or accounts receivable. It is an attractive option for firms with strong credit ratings, as it often offers lower interest rates compared to bank loans. In the context of VPN use cases, businesses might use commercial paper to fund technology investments, including securing reliable virtual private networks. This can help ensure secure remote access to corporate resources, enhance data protection, and maintain compliance with regulations. As organizations continue to adopt remote work policies, the role of secure communication channels supported by funding mechanisms like commercial paper becomes increasingly relevant.

Examples

  • A corporation issues commercial paper to finance its short-term operational needs.
  • A tech company uses proceeds from commercial paper to invest in VPN infrastructure for enhanced security.

Additional Information

  • Commercial paper is typically sold at a discount and redeemed at face value.
  • Investors in commercial paper include money market funds, banks, and corporations.
