Builder's risk insurance

A type of property insurance that covers buildings under construction.

Description

Builder's risk insurance is a specialized form of property insurance designed to cover structures while they are being built or renovated. This insurance protects against various risks that can lead to financial loss, such as fire, theft, vandalism, and certain weather-related damages. It typically covers the building itself, as well as materials, fixtures, and equipment on-site. Builder's risk insurance is essential for construction projects, as it provides peace of mind to builders, contractors, and property owners, ensuring that unforeseen incidents do not lead to significant financial setbacks. This insurance is usually purchased for a specific term, often corresponding to the duration of the construction project, and can be extended if the project takes longer than anticipated. It is important to understand the specific conditions and exclusions of a builder's risk policy, as coverage can vary significantly between insurers. Additionally, this insurance does not cover liability for injuries that occur on the job site or damage to adjacent properties, which may require separate liability coverage.

Examples

  • Coverage for a new residential home being built, protecting against fire and theft.
  • Insurance for a commercial building under renovation, covering materials and equipment.

Additional Information

  • Typically required by lenders before financing a construction project.
  • Can include coverage for soft costs like delayed project completion due to covered losses.
