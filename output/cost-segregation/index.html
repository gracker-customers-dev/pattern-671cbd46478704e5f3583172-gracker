Cost segregation

A tax strategy that allows for the accelerated depreciation of certain property components.

Description

Cost segregation is a tax strategy primarily used by real estate owners to maximize cash flow by accelerating depreciation deductions on property. This involves identifying and separating the costs of various components of a property, such as land improvements, personal property, and buildings, into different asset classes with varying depreciation schedules. By segregating costs, property owners can take advantage of shorter depreciation periods, often as short as 5, 7, or 15 years, instead of the standard 27.5 or 39 years for residential and commercial properties, respectively. This accelerated depreciation can significantly reduce taxable income in the early years of property ownership, leading to substantial tax savings. These savings can then be reinvested into the business or property for further development, enhancing overall financial performance. Cost segregation studies typically require the expertise of tax professionals or engineering firms to ensure compliance with IRS guidelines and to maximize the benefits of the strategy.

Examples

  • A commercial building owner reclassifies components like HVAC systems and flooring to achieve faster depreciation.
  • A residential rental property owner conducts a cost segregation study to reduce tax burden and increase cash flow.

Additional Information

  • Cost segregation can be applied to new constructions, renovations, and acquisitions.
  • Professional cost segregation studies can be costly but often result in significant tax savings.
