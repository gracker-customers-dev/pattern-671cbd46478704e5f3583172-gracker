Construction mortgage

A type of loan specifically for financing the construction of a new home or property.

Description

A construction mortgage is a specialized type of loan that provides the necessary funds to build a new home or commercial property. Unlike traditional mortgages, which finance an existing property, construction mortgages are designed to cover the costs associated with constructing a building. These loans typically have a shorter term and may be structured as a line of credit that releases funds in stages, or 'draws,' as construction progresses. Borrowers often need to provide a detailed construction plan and budget, as well as work with a licensed contractor. The lender may require inspections at various stages to ensure that the work is being completed as planned. Once construction is complete, the construction mortgage can often be converted into a traditional mortgage, allowing the borrower to secure a long-term loan at a fixed or variable interest rate.

Examples

  • A homeowner secures a construction mortgage to build their dream house from the ground up.
  • A developer uses a construction mortgage to finance the building of a multi-family apartment complex.

Additional Information

  • Interest rates on construction mortgages may be higher than traditional mortgages due to the increased risk involved.
  • Borrowers should be prepared for a rigorous application process that includes submitting detailed plans and financial documentation.
