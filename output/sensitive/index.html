Sensitive

Refers to data or information that requires protection due to its confidential nature.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, the term 'sensitive' pertains to information that, if compromised, could lead to unauthorized access, data breaches, or significant privacy violations. This includes personal data, financial information, intellectual property, and any other data deemed critical to an individual or organization. VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) are often employed to create a secure tunnel for internet traffic, ensuring that sensitive information is encrypted and shielded from potential eavesdroppers, particularly when using public Wi-Fi networks. VPNs help to mask the user's IP address, adding an additional layer of anonymity and security. Businesses frequently utilize VPNs to protect remote workers accessing sensitive corporate resources or databases. The definition of sensitive can vary based on legal regulations, industry standards, and organizational policies, making it essential for users to understand what constitutes sensitive data in their specific context.

Examples

  • Accessing online banking services securely while on public Wi-Fi.
  • Transmitting confidential corporate documents between remote offices.

Additional Information

  • Sensitive data may include personal identifiable information (PII) and protected health information (PHI).
  • Using a VPN can help meet compliance requirements for data protection in various industries.
