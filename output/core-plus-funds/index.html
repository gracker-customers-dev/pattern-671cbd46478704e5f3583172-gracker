Core-plus funds

Core-plus funds are a type of investment fund that combines core real estate holdings with additional riskier investments.

Description

Core-plus funds represent a strategy in real estate investment that allows investors to achieve stable income with some potential for capital appreciation. These funds primarily invest in core properties, which are typically high-quality, well-located assets with a history of stable cash flows. However, core-plus funds also allocate a portion of their capital to value-add or opportunistic investments, which may involve properties needing renovation, repositioning, or development. This hybrid approach allows investors to benefit from the steady income generated by core assets while also pursuing higher returns through the more aggressive investments. The risk profile of core-plus funds is usually higher than that of pure core funds due to the exposure to more volatile investments, but they also offer the potential for improved returns. As such, they are suitable for investors seeking a balanced approach to real estate investment, combining both stability and growth potential.

Examples

  • A fund investing in a well-leased office building while also acquiring a retail property for redevelopment.
  • A fund that holds a portfolio of stabilized multifamily properties and invests in a new construction project.

Additional Information

  • Core-plus funds typically have a longer investment horizon than core funds.
  • They may appeal to institutional investors looking for a blend of income and capital appreciation.
