Receipt

A document confirming the successful completion of a VPN session.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a receipt refers to a digital or electronic document generated at the end of a VPN session that confirms the successful connection and disconnection of the user from the virtual private network. This receipt may include various details such as the time of connection, duration of the session, IP address used, and the data transmitted during the session. It serves as a proof of service for users who may need to verify their VPN usage for security audits, compliance checks, or personal record-keeping. Receipts can also help organizations track user activity, ensure compliance with data protection regulations, and identify any potential unauthorized access. Furthermore, some VPN providers may offer receipts to enhance transparency, thereby building trust with their users regarding how their data is handled and secured during VPN sessions.

Examples

  • Receipt showing connection details for user authentication purposes.
  • Receipt documenting data usage for compliance with corporate security policies.

Additional Information

  • Receipts may be automatically emailed to users after a session.
  • Some VPN services provide detailed logs as part of the receipt for audit trails.
