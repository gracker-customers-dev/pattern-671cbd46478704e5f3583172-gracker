Title insurance policy

A title insurance policy is a form of indemnity insurance that protects property buyers and lenders from financial loss due to defects in a title to real property.

Description

A title insurance policy is a crucial document in real estate transactions, ensuring that buyers and lenders are protected against potential claims or disputes over property ownership. This type of insurance covers various risks, including errors in public records, undisclosed liens, fraud, and other issues that may arise after the purchase of a property. Unlike other types of insurance that require ongoing premiums, title insurance is typically a one-time payment made at closing. The policy guarantees that the title is clear at the time of sale, and if any issues arise later, the insurer will cover the legal costs and losses incurred. This provides peace of mind for buyers and lenders, ensuring that their investment is safeguarded against unforeseen title defects. The scope of coverage can vary, and it is essential for buyers to review the policy details to understand what is included and any exclusions that may apply. Overall, a title insurance policy is an essential safeguard in real estate transactions.

Examples

  • A buyer discovers an undisclosed lien on the property after closing, and the title insurance policy covers the cost of resolving the lien.
  • A lender finds out that a previous owner had committed fraud, and the title insurance policy protects them against financial loss associated with that fraud.

Additional Information

  • Title insurance is typically required by lenders before approving a mortgage.
  • There are two main types of title insurance policies: owner's policies and lender's policies.
