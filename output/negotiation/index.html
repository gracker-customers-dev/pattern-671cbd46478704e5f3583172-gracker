Negotiation

The process of establishing parameters for secure communication between VPN endpoints.

Description

In the context of Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), negotiation refers to the process by which two VPN endpoints agree on the parameters of their secure connection. This includes the selection of encryption protocols, authentication methods, and key exchange mechanisms. During the negotiation phase, both parties communicate their capabilities and preferences to reach a common understanding that ensures secure data transmission. The most common protocols used for this purpose are IKE (Internet Key Exchange) for IPsec VPNs and TLS (Transport Layer Security) for SSL VPNs. Effective negotiation is crucial as it lays the groundwork for establishing a secure tunnel, protecting the integrity and confidentiality of the data being transmitted. A successful negotiation process not only enhances security but also optimizes performance by selecting the most suitable protocols and settings for the specific network environment and use case. Overall, negotiation is an essential step in establishing a secure VPN connection, ensuring that both parties can communicate safely over potentially insecure networks.

Examples

  • Negotiation of IKE parameters for IPsec VPN connections.
  • TLS handshake negotiation in SSL VPNs.

Additional Information

  • Negotiation can involve multiple rounds of communication before agreement.
  • Some VPNs support automatic negotiation to simplify the process.
