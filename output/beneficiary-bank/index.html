Beneficiary bank

The financial institution that receives funds on behalf of the beneficiary in a transaction.

Description

In the context of financial transactions, particularly in international payments, a beneficiary bank is the bank where the recipient of funds holds their account. When a payment is initiated, the funds are transferred from the sender’s bank (the remitter bank) to the beneficiary bank, which credits the beneficiary's account with the received amount. This process is crucial for ensuring that the intended recipient receives their money securely and efficiently. Beneficiary banks often have relationships with various correspondent banks to facilitate these transactions across borders. In the context of VPN use cases, beneficiary banks may utilize VPNs to secure their communications and transactions, ensuring that sensitive financial data is protected during the transfer process. The use of VPNs helps in mitigating risks related to data breaches and enhances the privacy of financial transactions, which is paramount in maintaining customer trust and regulatory compliance.

Examples

  • A beneficiary bank in a cross-border wire transfer receiving funds from a corporate client.
  • A beneficiary bank that processes remittances for individuals sending money to family members abroad.

Additional Information

  • Beneficiary banks may charge fees for receiving funds, depending on the type of transaction.
  • The efficiency and speed of transactions can vary based on the relationships between beneficiary banks and correspondent banks.
