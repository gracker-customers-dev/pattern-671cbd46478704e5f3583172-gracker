Execution date

The date on which a VPN connection is established or a specific VPN-related action is implemented.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, the execution date refers to the specific date and time when a VPN session is initiated or when a VPN policy, configuration, or command is executed. This date is crucial for tracking the performance and effectiveness of VPN operations, as well as for compliance and auditing purposes. For instance, when a company decides to implement a new VPN solution or update its existing configuration, the execution date marks the start of these changes. It is also essential for recording the duration of the VPN usage, ensuring that corporate policies regarding data protection and remote access are adhered to. By maintaining a log of execution dates, organizations can analyze usage patterns, troubleshoot issues, and optimize their VPN infrastructure based on historical data. Furthermore, the execution date can be significant in legal contexts, where the timing of data access or transfer over a VPN may need to be verified.

Examples

  • A company implements a new VPN policy on March 15, 2023.
  • An IT department establishes a VPN connection for remote access on April 1, 2023.

Additional Information

  • Execution dates are often logged in system records for auditing.
  • They can help identify trends in VPN usage over time.
