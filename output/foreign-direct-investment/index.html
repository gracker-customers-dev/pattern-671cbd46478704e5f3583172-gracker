Foreign direct investment

Investment made by a company or individual in one country in business interests in another country.

Description

Foreign direct investment (FDI) refers to the investment made by a company or individual in one country into business interests located in another country. This typically involves acquiring a substantial degree of influence or control over the foreign business operation, often defined as owning at least 10% of the company. FDI can take various forms, including establishing new business operations, acquiring existing businesses, or investing in joint ventures. In the context of VPN use cases, FDI can be significant as it often involves multinational corporations that require secure and reliable communication channels to manage and operate their overseas investments. VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) provide these companies with the ability to securely connect to their home networks while accessing international branches, protecting sensitive data, and maintaining compliance with local regulations. As businesses expand globally, leveraging VPN technology becomes crucial for safeguarding corporate communications and data integrity across borders.

Examples

  • A U.S. technology company establishes a research and development facility in India.
  • A European automotive manufacturer acquires a stake in a Chinese electric vehicle startup.

Additional Information

  • FDI can lead to technology transfer and knowledge sharing between countries.
  • Countries often provide incentives to attract foreign direct investment, such as tax breaks or reduced regulations.
