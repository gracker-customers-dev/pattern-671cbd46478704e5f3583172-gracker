Wave

A concept referring to the dynamic and evolving state of VPN usage within organizations.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'Wave' signifies the patterns of adoption, implementation, and usage of Virtual Private Networks across various organizations and industries. As remote work becomes more prevalent and cybersecurity threats evolve, the demand for VPNs has surged. Organizations experience waves of increased VPN usage during specific events, such as global crises or the launch of new remote work policies. Each 'wave' can be characterized by different user needs, technological advancements, and security requirements. For instance, early waves may focus on basic secure remote access, while later waves might incorporate advanced features like split tunneling, multi-factor authentication, and zero-trust networking principles. Understanding these waves helps organizations adapt their VPN solutions to meet changing demands and ensures that they effectively support their users in a secure manner.

Examples

  • Wave of increased VPN adoption during the COVID-19 pandemic as companies shifted to remote work.
  • Evolving usage patterns where organizations implement more robust VPN solutions in response to rising cybersecurity threats.

Additional Information

  • The concept of 'waves' can also apply to the technological evolution of VPNs, such as the shift from traditional VPNs to cloud-based solutions.
  • Monitoring VPN usage waves can provide insights into user behavior and inform future IT strategies.
