Accessory dwelling units

Small, secondary housing units on a single-family residential lot.

Description

Accessory dwelling units (ADUs) are secondary housing units that are located on the same lot as a primary single-family residence. These units can take various forms, including basement apartments, garage conversions, or new constructions that stand independently in the yard. ADUs serve multiple purposes, such as providing affordable housing options, accommodating family members, or generating rental income for homeowners. In the context of urban planning and housing policy, ADUs are increasingly recognized for their potential to increase housing density without altering the character of neighborhoods. They allow homeowners to maximize the use of their property while contributing to the availability of housing in densely populated areas. With the rise of the sharing economy and changing family dynamics, ADUs have gained popularity as flexible living spaces that can accommodate a variety of needs, from housing elderly relatives to serving as short-term rental units. Local governments often encourage the development of ADUs through zoning reforms and incentive programs to address housing shortages.

Examples

  • A converted garage serving as a rental unit.
  • A basement apartment occupied by elderly family members.

Additional Information

  • ADUs can help alleviate housing shortages in urban areas.
  • Regulations regarding ADUs vary significantly by municipality.
