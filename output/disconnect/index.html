Disconnect

The action of terminating a VPN connection.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'Disconnect' refers to the process of terminating an active VPN (Virtual Private Network) connection. When a user disconnects from a VPN, their internet traffic is no longer routed through the secure tunnel established by the VPN service. This action can be taken for various reasons, such as needing to access local networks or services that require a direct internet connection without the VPN's encryption and routing. Disconnecting can also be a part of troubleshooting processes, where a user may need to reconnect to the VPN to resolve connectivity issues or to switch to a different server. Additionally, users may choose to disconnect if they are finished with their secure browsing session or if they wish to use a different network configuration. It is important for users to understand the implications of disconnecting, as it can expose their internet traffic to potential monitoring or interception, especially when using untrusted networks.

Examples

  • A user disconnects from the VPN to access a local printer on their office network.
  • A traveler disconnects from the VPN to improve internet speed while using a public Wi-Fi network.

Additional Information

  • Some VPN services offer automatic disconnect features to enhance security.
  • Users should be aware of the risks of disconnecting from a VPN on unsecured networks.
