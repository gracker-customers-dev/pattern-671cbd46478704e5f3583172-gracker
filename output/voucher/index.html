Voucher

A voucher is a digital or physical token that grants access to a VPN service for a specified duration or data limit.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a voucher typically serves as a prepaid means to access a VPN service. Users can purchase these vouchers to gain temporary access to a VPN without needing to commit to a full subscription. Vouchers can be particularly useful for users who require short-term VPN access, such as travelers, occasional users, or businesses needing to provide temporary secure connections for employees. When a user redeems a voucher, they are usually given a unique code or link that activates their VPN access. This access can be tied to a specific time frame (e.g., 24 hours) or data usage limit (e.g., 10GB). Vouchers facilitate a flexible payment model that appeals to users who may not need a long-term commitment, allowing them to pay only for what they use. This model also allows VPN providers to attract new users who can test services without a substantial financial commitment.

Examples

  • A traveler purchases a one-week VPN voucher for secure internet access while abroad.
  • A company issues vouchers to employees for temporary remote work access to secure company resources.

Additional Information

  • Vouchers can often be purchased online or at retail locations.
  • Some VPN services may offer discounts or promotions for bulk voucher purchases.
