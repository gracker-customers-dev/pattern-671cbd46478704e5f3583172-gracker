Connected building systems

Integrated systems within a building that communicate and share data over a network.

Description

Connected building systems refer to the various integrated technologies and systems within a building that interact and exchange information through a network. These systems can include heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC), lighting, security, and energy management systems. The integration of these systems allows for enhanced monitoring, control, and management of building operations. In the context of VPN use cases, connected building systems can securely transmit data over the internet to remote management platforms. This enables facility managers to monitor and control building systems from anywhere, ensuring energy efficiency, security, and comfort. VPNs provide a secure communication channel that protects sensitive building data from unauthorized access while allowing for real-time access to operational data. Furthermore, connected building systems can facilitate the implementation of smart building technologies, where data analytics and IoT (Internet of Things) devices play a crucial role in enhancing operational efficiency and occupant experience.

Examples

  • HVAC systems that can be monitored and adjusted remotely via a secure VPN connection.
  • Smart lighting systems that use data from occupancy sensors to optimize energy use while being accessible over a VPN.

Additional Information

  • VPNs enhance the security of data exchanged between building systems and management applications.
  • Integration of connected systems can lead to significant cost savings through improved efficiency and resource management.
