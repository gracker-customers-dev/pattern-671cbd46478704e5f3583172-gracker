Low rise

A VPN use case involving minimal reliance on complex security measures or extensive infrastructure.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, 'Low rise' refers to scenarios where the implementation of a Virtual Private Network is straightforward, typically involving fewer technical complexities and a focus on basic security needs. Organizations or individuals using low rise VPNs often seek to secure their internet connections without the need for extensive configuration or high-end security features. This could involve connecting to public Wi-Fi networks securely or accessing geo-restricted content with basic encryption. Low rise VPNs are particularly popular among small businesses and casual users who need simple solutions to protect their online activities without the overhead of managing advanced VPN infrastructures. These use cases emphasize ease of use and quick setup, making them accessible to users with limited technical expertise. While they may not provide the highest level of security compared to enterprise-grade solutions, they serve essential purposes for users looking to enhance their online privacy and circumvent basic restrictions.

Examples

  • Using a VPN to securely browse the internet on public Wi-Fi.
  • Accessing streaming services that are geo-blocked in certain regions.

Additional Information

  • Low rise VPNs may not include features like split tunneling or multi-factor authentication.
  • They are often subscription-based services that provide user-friendly applications for various devices.
