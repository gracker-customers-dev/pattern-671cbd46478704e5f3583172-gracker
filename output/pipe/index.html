Pipe

A virtual connection tunnel established for secure data transmission over a VPN.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'Pipe' refers to a secure, virtual tunnel that facilitates the transmission of data between two or more endpoints. This tunnel encapsulates the data packets to ensure confidentiality and integrity, preventing unauthorized access or interception during transit. Pipes can be established over various protocols, such as OpenVPN, IPsec, or L2TP, each providing different levels of security and performance. Organizations utilize pipes for a multitude of reasons, including remote access to corporate networks, connecting branch offices, or securing communications over public networks. The use of pipes allows for the extension of a private network across public infrastructures while maintaining the necessary security measures. Additionally, different configurations such as site-to-site or remote access setups can be implemented, depending on the specific needs of the users or organizations involved. Overall, pipes are a fundamental concept in VPN technology, enabling secure and reliable communication in today's interconnected digital landscape.

Examples

  • Remote employees accessing company resources securely via a VPN pipe.
  • Branch offices connecting to a central corporate network through site-to-site VPN pipes.

Additional Information

  • VPN pipes can be encrypted to enhance security against eavesdropping.
  • Different types of VPN protocols can affect the performance and security of the pipe.
