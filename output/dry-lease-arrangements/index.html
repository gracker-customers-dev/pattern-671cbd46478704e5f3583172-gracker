Dry lease arrangements

A type of leasing arrangement where only the use of a resource is provided without additional services.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, dry lease arrangements refer to scenarios where a company leases network infrastructure or bandwidth without any accompanying management or operational services. This means that the lessee has control over the leased resources and is responsible for their operation, configuration, and maintenance. Dry leasing is often contrasted with wet leasing, which includes additional support services such as technical management, monitoring, and maintenance. Organizations opt for dry leases when they have the necessary expertise and resources to manage the leased infrastructure effectively, allowing for greater customization and control over their network operations. This arrangement is particularly useful for businesses with specific, high-demand applications that require tailored solutions or for those looking to minimize operational costs by leveraging their existing IT staff and resources.

Examples

  • A company leases dedicated bandwidth from an ISP to set up its own VPN without additional technical support.
  • An organization rents server hardware for hosting its own VPN service, managing the entire setup independently.

Additional Information

  • Dry lease arrangements typically require more in-house expertise than wet leases.
  • These arrangements can be more cost-effective for organizations with sufficient technical capabilities.
