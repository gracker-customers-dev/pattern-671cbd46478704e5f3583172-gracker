Federation

A system that allows multiple independent entities to share resources and services securely over a VPN.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, federation refers to the establishment of a framework that enables different organizations to collaborate and share resources while maintaining their own independent identities and control. This is particularly relevant in environments where multiple companies or divisions require secure access to shared data or applications without needing to merge their IT infrastructures. Federation typically involves the use of federated identity management, where users from one organization can access resources in another organization using their existing credentials, facilitated by trust relationships established between the entities. This not only streamlines access management but also enhances security by reducing the number of credentials that users need to manage. Federated VPN solutions can provide seamless connectivity across organizational boundaries, enabling secure communication and collaboration while adhering to compliance and regulatory requirements. Federations are commonly used in cloud services, multi-tenant applications, and consortia of businesses that require collaborative efforts while safeguarding their proprietary information.

Examples

  • A healthcare consortium sharing patient data securely among various hospitals and clinics.
  • Collaborative research projects that allow universities to access each other's resources and data while maintaining their own security protocols.

Additional Information

  • Federation can simplify user access by employing Single Sign-On (SSO) mechanisms.
  • It enhances security by establishing trust frameworks that govern how identities and data are shared between federated entities.
