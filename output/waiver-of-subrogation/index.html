Waiver of subrogation

A contractual agreement where one party relinquishes their right to seek compensation from another party for a loss covered by insurance.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a waiver of subrogation may come into play when organizations utilize Virtual Private Networks to secure their communications and data exchanges. This waiver typically means that if a third-party vendor or service provider, such as a VPN provider, experiences a loss or liability while providing services, the organization cannot pursue recovery from them for costs that would be covered by their own insurance. This is particularly relevant in contracts where the organization and the service provider agree to protect each other from certain types of claims. For example, if a data breach occurs while using a VPN service, the organization may have to rely on its insurance policy for coverage rather than seeking damages from the VPN provider. This arrangement helps foster trust and collaboration between parties, ensuring that both understand the risks involved and agree on how losses will be managed without placing blame on each other, thus streamlining operations and potentially lowering liability costs.

Examples

  • A company using a VPN service agrees not to hold the provider liable for any data breaches if they have insurance coverage.
  • A remote worker accesses corporate resources via a VPN and agrees to waive subrogation rights in case of a security incident.

Additional Information

  • Waivers of subrogation are common in contracts involving insurance and risk management.
  • These waivers can affect how claims are handled and may influence the pricing of insurance premiums.
