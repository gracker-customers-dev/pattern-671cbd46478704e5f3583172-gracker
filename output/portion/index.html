Portion

A portion refers to a segment of network traffic or data that is transmitted through a VPN.

Description

In the context of VPN use cases, a 'portion' can refer to specific segments of data or traffic that are routed through a VPN connection. This is particularly relevant in scenarios where only certain applications or types of data need the privacy and security that a VPN provides. For instance, a business might choose to route only sensitive data through the VPN while allowing other, less sensitive traffic to go directly through the internet. This selective routing enhances performance by reducing the load on the VPN and optimizing bandwidth usage. Additionally, users can configure their VPN clients to create rules that determine which portions of their internet traffic should be encrypted, allowing for a tailored approach to privacy and security. This flexibility is crucial for organizations that need to balance performance with security, as well as for individual users who may want to access geo-restricted content while keeping other browsing activities unencumbered by the VPN.

Examples

  • Routing only work-related applications through the VPN while personal browsing is done outside of it.
  • Using split tunneling to direct streaming traffic through the internet while keeping sensitive transactions on the VPN.

Additional Information

  • Portions can be defined by network protocols, applications, or IP addresses.
  • VPN configurations can be adjusted to optimize performance based on the identified portions of traffic.
