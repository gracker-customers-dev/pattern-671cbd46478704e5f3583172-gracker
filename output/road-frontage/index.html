Road frontage

The length of property that runs along a road or street.

Description

In real estate, 'road frontage' refers to the portion of a property that directly abuts a road or street. This measurement is crucial because properties with significant road frontage are often more desirable and can command higher prices due to increased visibility and accessibility. It is an important factor for various use cases, particularly in commercial real estate where businesses benefit from high traffic exposure. Properties with substantial road frontage are ideal for retail locations, restaurants, and other businesses that rely on foot and vehicle traffic. Additionally, road frontage can affect zoning regulations and potential uses of a property, influencing how the land can be developed. In urban planning and real estate development, understanding road frontage helps developers assess property value and layout considerations, ensuring that projects align with community needs and traffic patterns. Overall, road frontage plays a vital role in property valuation and development strategies.

Examples

  • A retail store located on a busy main street with 100 feet of road frontage.
  • A residential property with 50 feet of road frontage on a suburban cul-de-sac.

Additional Information

  • Properties with more road frontage may have a competitive advantage in visibility.
  • Zoning laws may dictate the minimum required road frontage for certain types of developments.
